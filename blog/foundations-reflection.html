Foundations Reflection

Using the journal findings and the high-level overview of the core learnings covered in the foundations. Here are my reflections on the journey so far.



What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

Something I had learnt about myself reflecting on the very early part of introducing myself and my values is that my background and past experiences in my family and environment have such a huge impact on my day to day. Learning more about my strengths and weaknesses has made me want to double down on my skills and want to work on my weaknesses.

Overall it has been very helpful to reflect on my thoughts during each sprint. Putting them in words through daily journaling gives me time to really think about what I have done for the day and sets me in the right frame of mind for the next steps.


What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values we hold close influence our day to day decisions and even in what we do. They are very important to understand what motivates someone for their actions. Empathy and self-awareness help connect and communicate with others and build meaningful relationships. They play a huge role in building trust with others including colleagues who we will be working with on different projects. As we have learnt already EQ plays a huge part not only understanding but also influencing people towards a common goal.


What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

With so much emphasis on IQ specially in a technical field like software development EQ is often overlooked. It has that perception that you either have high EQ or you don’t. But like many other skills it is definitely something you can get better in with deliberate, consistent and conscious efforts.


What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Sharing personal stories with the team has been the most challenging aspect of the core learnings.

Making sure to carve out time and prioritising mental fitness and wellbeing has been difficult at times too.


Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I think Dev academy’s focus IQ & EQ differentiates the graduates from other bootcamps.

"Hire for fit coach for skills"

This is something I have learnt after 2-3 years managing my own team – someone who has good communication, fits the cultural requirements for the team & has high EQ will always outperform someone who has high IQ but very low EQ.


Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

Definitely don’t think it has been a waste of time so far and am looking forward to how being in the bootcamp will further my EQ working in a team environment.

So far I think we have been in an exploration phase learning more about ourselves, more about the importance of the core skills we need and getting a good foundation and mindset for more.
