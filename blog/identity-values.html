Identity, Values and Strengths

here's a blog to share a bit about me.

  • Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

    I recall one summer holiday when I was 17 - me, my mother and brother were supposed to visit dad in our Chinese factory and meet him after 6-8 months. An important sales person from our Mumbai showroom had unexpectedly left for an extended annual leave during the busy months. I remember to volunteer by cancelling my summer holidays and stay back to help out. Looking back it was not a difficult decision for me as I had seen members of my family make similar sacrifices for the collective good.
    I was not asked or expected to forgo my summer holidays and could have easily let someone else pickup the work but my values around showing dedication to the collective good helped with my decision.

  • Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

    I belong to a trading community in India. Most of my family & ancestors including my parents have been self-employed. I have always been encouraged to get involved in the family business. That exposure has taught me a lot about hard-work, diligence, integrity and putting collective good over self. Years have passed and my involvement in the family business has only grown until this year when I decided to explore new opportunities.

  • Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

    I have taken “16 personalities test” and it turns out my personality type is Architech INTJ-T. Architects are one of the rarest personality types - and the most capable. Strengths include - being rational, informed, independent, determined & creative.
    My strength to stay calm at the face of an overwhelming challenge - turning to outside resources to identify common themes for a solution will help me tackle new challenges when I inevitably find myself in the deep end in my new career.

  • Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

    Architect personality type can be arrogant, dismissive of emotions, overly critical, combative and socially clueless as per the “16 personalities” test. After reading on these short-comings I can definitely relate and can recall instances when I was let down.
    I tend to get bored with mundane repetitive tasks a lot and like to look at the big picture and strategise. This has been an issue when I am not able to perform tasks that I do not see too much value in. I believe this could be an issue for me in the initial years of my career as I do not realistically expect to be in a highly decision making position as a career switcher.
    To overcome this issue in the past, I volunteer to help out with tasks that excite me even though they were clearly outside my responsibilities. This strategy has paid-off in many instances and landed me in not so ideal situations. I have learnt from them and know what I need to look out for and what to stay away from in the future.

  • Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

    As an ecommerce manager in my most recent role my responsibilities were spread wide. I have found resistance from team leaders to implement new processes for various tasks.
    I tried to discuss the issues with them so they can see the bigger picture in implementing new processes. My primary task would be to get team leaders on board, educate them around reasons for change and lastly to provide enough training for them to implement the changes within their teams.
    In future my goal would be to follow up on their implementation progress more closely.
