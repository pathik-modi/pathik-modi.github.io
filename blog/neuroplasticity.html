Research Neuroplasticity

Neuro plasticity to begin with is a mash-up of two words: Neural: relating to nervous system & Plasticity: the capacity to adapt. It refers to the brains ability to change itself. It is the process in which the brain forms new neurons and lays down new connections between neurons, in response to learning, in order to form a pathway to repear the learned behaviour.

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.
Neuroplasticity is the ability for these connections to brain and body to change in response to experience. It is the fundamental to learning, memory and recovery from brain injury. It plays a huge role in developmental growth. It allows the brain to fine-tune connections based on experiences and environmental stimuli, shaping cognitive and emotional behaviour. It can help motivate pursue personal growth, self-improvement and development. It encourages the idea that personal growth and improvement are possible at any age given the right tools and environment.

Research Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.
It is a way of viewing challenges and setbacks. People who have a growth mindset believe even if they struggle with a certain skill, theur abilities aren’t set in stone. They thing that with work, their skills can improve overtime.
It is super relevant to the current situation while we are all learning new skills and are bound to hit a wall in that process. Having growth mindset will help us overcome and look past those challenges in a bigger picture and take them as a learning experience as opposed to obstacles.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?
The ted talk surprised me in many places specially referring to the results that kids saw with just seeing not passed yet. Yet being the keyword here. It showed them that they still have room to improve and it is not the end yet. There is a popular dialogue in an Indian movie that comes to mind (I might be paraphrasing here) but it goes something like it’s not over until it’s over.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?
Looking at the bigger picture basically for the journey, I guess that’s why they say it’s a journey. Remembering that it’s a marathon and not a sprint. Taking a break when things get tough to reframe the challenge as a growing opportunity. Reminding myself that it is supposed to be difficult whenever I come across a tough challenge. Take a break to divert my mind and come back to take a stab at it again. Keep trying until it becomes easy and then find something else to challenge and repeat the process.
